ProGlove - Wearables for Industry

Utilizing Northeastern University's extensive experiental learning program, I decided to self-develop my second co-op by combining my passion for innovation and design with electrical engineering in a small company setting. My passion led me to a company called ProGlove which is a start-up founded in Munich, Germany by former BMW and IDEO employees developing wearable electronics for industry. Their smart gloves intergrate hands-free barcode scanning and wireless conectivity to enable employees in logistics to work faster and more efficiently on manufacturing lines. The combination of working on Industry 4.0 and Internet of Things products with a human-centered design based approach has significantly shaped my career goals and aspirations.

München, Deutschland

On May 5, 2016 I stepped off the plane at Flughafen München with two suitcases and a backpack. For the next 8 months, the state of Bavaria in southeastern Germany would become my home. I didn't know any German, have any permanent living arrangements, or know a single person in the city. In retrospect, it was absolutely crazy for taking an engineering internship halfway around the world, but I have learned more about the world and myself than I could have ever imagined.
